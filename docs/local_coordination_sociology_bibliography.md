# Sociology of Local Coordination Systems

## Working Bibliography

> **Central question:** Under what social, spatial, economic, institutional, technological, and cultural conditions can geographically proximate people develop durable capacities for voluntary collective action and exchange?

## How to use this file

- Change `[ ]` to `[x]` when a reading is completed.
- Add an Obsidian link after an entry when a reading note exists: `[[Author Year — Short Title]]`.
- Use `#anchor`, `#current`, `#case`, or `#methods` tags inside notes rather than expanding this bibliography indefinitely.
- Preprints and workshop reports are explicitly labeled. They should be evaluated differently from peer-reviewed journal articles and established monographs.

# Reading-list notes

- **Total unique entries in this draft:** 84

- The list should eventually be reduced to roughly **45–55 required works**, with the remainder designated supplemental.

# Open editorial questions

1. Should the primary empirical focus remain U.S. neighborhood institutions, or should the examination deliberately include more cases from the Global South?
2. Should housing and landlord–tenant power become a larger independent field?
3. Should the experimental-community section include intentional communities, monasteries, religious houses, hacker houses, artist collectives, and cooperative housing as comparative cases?
4. How much formal network analysis and institutional-analysis methodology is necessary?
5. Which readings directly alter the design of the three-property system, rather than only providing broad intellectual context?
